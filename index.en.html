Why Džiugas Matters

Džiugas Since 1924.

Wheels are aged for 12 to 120 months, with regular turning and inspection. Since 2019, the product has been protected under EU PGI.

The Challenge

Why Now?

Three pressure points make AI quality control a strategic necessity.

Ripening Vulnerability

Thousands of wheels age at once. Small environmental shifts can affect a full batch.

Up to 10 years of value built into each wheel

Labor Pressure

Labor shortages persist. Manual inspection differs across shifts, so grading is inconsistent.

Growing workforce gap across EU dairy

Batch Loss Risk

A single undetected mold event can spread to adjacent wheels and damage the batch.

Cascade failure across shared cellars

1

Capture

A 4K camera captures each wheel surface without workflow disruption.

2

The model detects cracks, mold spots, discoloration, and deformation.

Validation & Plan

Industry Proof & Pilot Plan

The same technology model is already deployed in European cheese ripening — and the results are documented.

Industry Reference

Eberle × Gebr. Baldauf Case Study

Gebr. Baldauf, a traditional Allgäu dairy (est. 1862), commissioned Eberle Automatische Systeme to automate cheese ripening inspection. The system uses a mobile robot with 4K cameras and MVTec HALCON deep learning to detect mold, cracks, and discoloration on every wheel.

The deep learning model was trained to distinguish true defects from natural rind variation — the same challenge Džiugas faces with its long-aged wheels.

Proposed Roadmap

Džiugas Pilot Plan

Phase 1 — Proof of Concept 3 months

Single ripening cellar. Camera installation, initial model training with Džiugas wheel imagery, baseline defect detection validation.

Phase 2 — Scale 6 months

Expand to multiple cellars. Refine model across all aging stages (Mild through Extra Aged). Integrate alerting with existing workflows.

Phase 3 — Full Integration 12 months

Facility-wide deployment. Per-wheel digital traceability. Dashboard integration for production management. Export compliance documentation.

Success Metrics

  • Defect detection vs. manual baseline
  • False positives below operating threshold
  • Labor hours saved per cycle
  • Batch waste reduction
